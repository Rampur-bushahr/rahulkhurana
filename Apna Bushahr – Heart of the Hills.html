Exploring Rampur Bushahr: The Hidden Gem of Himachal Pradesh

Nestled along the banks of the Satluj River, Rampur Bushahr is a quaint town in Himachal Pradesh that blends history, culture, and breathtaking landscapes. Located about 130 km from Shimla, Rampur Bushahr has long been a significant trade hub on the ancient Silk Route, connecting India with Tibet.

A Glimpse into History

Rampur Bushahr was once the capital of the princely state of Bushahr. The town flourished during the rule of the Bushahr dynasty, with Raja Kehri Singh (1639–1696) being one of its most notable rulers. His legacy of promoting arts, culture, and trade has left a lasting impact on the region.

Padam Palace: A Royal Marvel

The Padam Palace, built in 1917 by Raja Padam Singh, is a stunning example of traditional Himachali architecture. With its intricate woodwork and striking façade, the palace stands as a symbol of the town’s regal past. Though not open to the public, visitors can admire its beauty from the outside.

Cultural Extravaganza: Lavi Fair

Rampur Bushahr is famous for hosting the Lavi Fair, a four-day trade festival held in November. Traders from Kinnaur, Tibet, and nearby regions gather here to exchange wool, pashmina, dry fruits, and other goods. The fair is a vibrant celebration that showcases local culture, folk dances, and music.

Must-Visit Attractions

  1. Satluj River Views: Take a stroll along the banks of the Satluj River and soak in the serene atmosphere.
  2. Dumgir Monastery: A peaceful place that offers spiritual solace and a glimpse into Tibetan culture.

3 . Hattu Peak: About 30 km away, this spot provides panoramic views of snow-capped peaks and lush valleys.

Adventure and Trekking

For adventure lovers, Rampur Bushahr serves as a base for treks to Kinnaur, Sarahan, and other nearby regions. Whether it’s exploring apple orchards or traversing mountain trails, this town offers a perfect escape for nature enthusiasts.

Local Cuisine: A Taste of Tradition

Don’t miss out on trying local Himachali delicacies such as:

  • Siddu: A steamed bread stuffed with poppy seeds or walnuts.
  • Chha Gosht: A flavorful lamb curry prepared with yogurt and spices.
  • Babru: A fried bread served with tangy chutney.

How to Reach Rampur Bushahr

  • By Road: Well-connected via NH-5, regular buses and taxis ply from Shimla and other nearby towns.
  • By Train: The nearest railhead is at Shimla, about 130 km away.
  • By Air: The nearest airport is Jubbarhatti Airport, Shimla.

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Rampur Bushahr is between March and June or September and November, when the weather is pleasant and perfect for exploring.
